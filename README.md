This is the machine learning project I created during the 2025 Cyber Security for Computer Science (CS4CS) program.  It compares a Naive Bayesian classifier, a feedforward neural network, and a TabNet classifier (a deep learning model that specializes in Tabular Data) in their ability to classify patients' lung cancer given certain clinical data.  In summary, the TabNet classifier performed the best with an accuracy of 98.63%, the feedforward neural network performed second-best with an accuracy of 96.55%, and the Naive Bayesian Classifier performed the worst with an accuracy of 90.55%.  Also, the TabNet Classifier and Naive Bayesian Classifier may prove more fruitful in the medical field due to their feature importance mechanisms.  These mechanisms indicate which features played more or less important roles in classification, effectively conveying their reasoning to physicians.
